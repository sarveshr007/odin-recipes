Pani Puri, also known as golgappa or puchka, is a beloved Indian street food that’s a burst of flavors in every bite. It consists of small, crispy, hollow puris filled with a spicy potato mixture, tangy tamarind chutney, and a zesty, spiced water (pani). The combination of sweet, sour, spicy, and savory makes it an irresistible snack. It’s my absolute favorite because each puri delivers a delightful explosion of taste, bringing back memories of enjoying it at bustling street stalls with friends.

Key Ingredients

  • 20-25 ready-made puris
  • 2 medium boiled potatoes (mashed)
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 2 cups cold water
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 1 green chili
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp black salt
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 2 tbsp sweet tamarind chutney
  • 1 tbsp boondi (optional) and salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling: Mix mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, 1 tsp chaat masala, 1 tsp roasted cumin powder, 1/2 tsp red chili powder, and salt in a bowl. Mash lightly to combine and set aside.
  2. Make the Pani (Spiced Water): Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chili, tamarind paste, black salt, chaat masala, and roasted cumin powder with 1/2 cup water into a smooth paste. Mix with remaining cold water, adjust seasoning, and chill for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Assemble the Pani Puri: Tap a puri to make a small hole, stuff with 1 tsp of the potato-chickpea filling, and add a few drops of tamarind chutney.
  4. Add the Pani and Serve: Dip the filled puri in the chilled pani or spoon the pani inside. Sprinkle with boondi if desired, and eat in one bite.
  5. Repeat and Enjoy: Prepare each puri fresh to maintain crispiness, serving with extra pani, filling, and chutney on the side.
